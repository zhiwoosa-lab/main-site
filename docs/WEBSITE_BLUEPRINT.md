# Website Vision

This website is not a traditional portfolio, blog, or product landing page.

It is a long-term digital life space.

The core idea of the website is:

> A continuously growing life tree that visualizes time, places, people, and events across an entire life.

The website should feel alive, evolving, and personal.

It is intended to become:
- a digital garden
- a life archive
- a personal study
- a timeline of growth
- an AI workspace
- a memory system
- a reflection of both external exploration and private family life

The website should continue growing over many years rather than feeling “finished.”

---

# Core Philosophy

The website is built around long-termism.

Growth, time, experimentation, success, failure, relationships, movement across countries, and changing ideas are all part of the system.

The website should feel:
- alive
- reflective
- quiet
- personal
- thoughtful
- organic
- human

Avoid:
- startup aesthetics
- aggressive tech branding
- noisy interfaces
- flashy AI visuals
- excessive animations
- cheap gradients
- overly futuristic cyberpunk styles

The site should feel timeless rather than trendy.

---

# The Tree System

The tree is the central structure of the website.

The tree is not decorative.

The tree itself IS the timeline.

---

## Tree Trunk

The trunk represents time.

Different life periods create different trunk sections.

Longer life stages should visually occupy more vertical space than shorter periods.

The tree continuously grows upward as new life periods are added.

The top of the tree always represents the current stage of life.

Scrolling downward means traveling backward into earlier life periods.

---

## Branches

Branches represent periods, directions, or chapters within life.

Examples:
- living in a country
- learning a field
- building a project
- entering a new phase of thinking
- relationships
- important life transitions

Branches can split naturally over time.

Some branches continue growing.
Some stop growing.

---

## Apples

Apples represent outcomes, experiences, projects, and major events.

### Red Apples
Represent:
- achievements
- meaningful outcomes
- completed goals
- successful growth

### Green Apples
Represent:
- lessons
- unfinished work
- failed experiments
- abandoned paths
- high-effort low-result periods

Apple size represents:
- time investment
- emotional importance
- long-term impact

Large green apples are important failures or painful lessons.
Small green apples are small unfinished attempts or short-lived experiments.

---

# Time System

Time is the primary navigation structure.

The website is fundamentally chronological.

The user begins at the current life stage.

Scrolling downward moves into the past.

The website should feel like:
- descending through memory
- revisiting earlier versions of life
- moving through changing environments over time

The experience should feel continuous rather than page-based.

---

# Place System

Places are not simple metadata tags.

Places define the environmental atmosphere of life stages.

Each major life period can have:
- different visual environments
- different background landscapes
- different colors
- different emotional tones

Examples:

South Africa:
- savanna
- warm light
- wildlife
- open landscapes

New York:
- dense urban atmosphere
- colder tones
- vertical city structures

Paris:
- softer textures
- stone architecture
- muted colors

The background world should subtly reflect the location without overwhelming the tree itself.

The tree trunk may visually react to the environment but should remain the central structure.

---

# Public and Private Layers

The website contains both public and private worlds.

Public areas:
- learning
- research
- AI experiments
- projects
- essays
- observations
- automation workflows

Private areas:
- family memories
- personal records
- sensitive life details

Private areas should require password-based access.

The separation between public and private should feel natural and respectful rather than hidden behind aggressive security UI.

---

# Core Dimensions

The website is organized around four dimensions:

## 1. Time
Life periods and chronology.

## 2. Place
Countries, cities, and environments connected to different life stages.

## 3. People
Especially family and important long-term relationships.

## 4. Events
Projects, lessons, transitions, experiments, successes, and failures.

These four dimensions should interconnect naturally.

---

# Homepage Philosophy

The homepage is not a traditional homepage.

It is the current visible section of the life tree.

Users should immediately encounter:
- the current life stage
- current projects
- current thoughts
- current direction of growth

The homepage should feel like entering a living world already in progress.

Scrolling downward reveals earlier stages of life.

The homepage should prioritize immersion over traditional navigation structures.

---

# Website Identity

The website is a hybrid of:
- digital garden
- archive
- study
- memory space
- life timeline
- personal world

It should not feel like:
- a resume
- a corporate portfolio
- a SaaS product page
- a social media profile

---

# Long-Term Goal

The long-term goal is to build a lifelong evolving digital environment that grows together with the person behind it.

The project should remain understandable, maintainable, and expandable for both humans and AI agents over time.
