# Design Philosophy

The website should feel like a living personal world rather than a modern tech product.

The experience should feel:
- organic
- calm
- reflective
- slow
- thoughtful
- human
- timeless

The visual system should support long-term emotional resonance rather than short-term visual excitement.

The design should feel alive, but never loud.

---

# Core Emotional Direction

The site should create the feeling of:
- entering a quiet study
- walking through memory
- revisiting earlier periods of life
- observing growth over time
- exploring a living archive

The experience should feel personal and intimate rather than performative.

Avoid:
- startup aesthetics
- corporate design language
- over-polished interfaces
- futuristic AI visuals
- trendy UI effects
- social media style layouts
- aggressive interactions

---

# Primary Visual Inspiration

The overall atmosphere should be inspired by:
- hand-drawn digital gardens
- sketchbook-like interfaces
- editorial layouts
- old notebooks
- archival systems
- natural growth patterns

Reference atmosphere:
- Maggie Appleton
- digital garden aesthetics
- illustrated personal archives

However, the website should evolve beyond existing digital garden examples and become its own visual language.

---

# Color Philosophy

The overall palette should be low saturation.

Colors should feel:
- warm
- soft
- aged
- natural
- atmospheric

The emotional center of the palette is:

## Beige

Beige is both a visual anchor and an emotional symbol connected to family and warmth.

The website should never feel stark white or overly dark.

Preferred tones:
- beige
- warm gray
- faded green
- muted brown
- soft gold
- dusty blue
- weathered charcoal

Avoid:
- neon colors
- strong gradients
- high saturation
- overly vibrant palettes
- pure black backgrounds

Different life periods may subtly shift environmental colors depending on place and emotional tone.

---

# Typography Philosophy

Typography should feel:
- literary
- calm
- readable
- archival
- thoughtful

The typography system should prioritize:
- long-form reading
- comfortable spacing
- slow reading rhythm
- elegant hierarchy

Avoid:
- futuristic fonts
- overly geometric styles
- aggressive boldness
- compressed typography

Text should feel like reading notes from a long-lived personal archive.

---

# Layout Philosophy

The layout should breathe.

Whitespace is important.

Pages should never feel crowded.

The experience should encourage:
- exploration
- reflection
- lingering
- slow scrolling

The design should feel more like a landscape than a dashboard.

---

# Tree Visualization Rules

The tree is the center of the experience.

The tree should feel:
- hand-drawn
- imperfect
- organic
- evolving

Avoid:
- hyper-realistic trees
- 3D rendering
- game-like visuals
- fantasy aesthetics

The tree should visually grow over time.

The trunk represents time itself.

Different periods of life may subtly alter:
- bark texture
- branch density
- environmental lighting
- surrounding atmosphere

---

# Animation Philosophy

Animation should support emotion and time.

Animation should never exist purely for spectacle.

Preferred animation qualities:
- slow
- subtle
- atmospheric
- natural
- breathing-like
- wind-like

Examples:
- slight branch movement
- slow drifting particles
- gradual environmental transitions
- soft lighting changes
- slow parallax movement

Avoid:
- bounce animations
- exaggerated transitions
- flashy motion
- fast-moving UI
- overly interactive gimmicks

Animation should feel like the passage of time.

---

# Scroll Philosophy

Scrolling represents movement through time.

The user starts at the current life stage.

Scrolling downward means:
- entering earlier periods
- revisiting memory
- descending through life history

The transition between periods should feel continuous and cinematic.

The website should feel vertically immersive rather than page-based.

---

# Illustration Rules

Illustrations should feel:
- hand-drawn
- sketch-like
- imperfect
- quiet
- personal

Preferred styles:
- vector sketching
- textured linework
- soft environmental illustration
- minimal color fills

Avoid:
- AI-generated poster aesthetics
- glossy rendering
- heavy realism
- over-detailing

Illustrations should support storytelling rather than dominate the screen.

---

# UI Philosophy

UI should remain secondary to atmosphere and storytelling.

The interface should almost disappear into the experience.

Preferred UI characteristics:
- minimal chrome
- soft edges
- restrained shadows
- subtle layering
- quiet transitions

Avoid:
- glassmorphism
- excessive cards
- heavy borders
- loud hover states
- dashboard aesthetics

The user should feel inside a world rather than operating software.

---

# Long-Term Design Goal

The website should age gracefully over many years.

The design system should support:
- continuous growth
- new life stages
- evolving environments
- expanding archives
- changing personal identity over time

The website should feel increasingly meaningful as more years are added to the tree.

# Homepage Visual Direction v1

The homepage should initially follow the visual direction of:

`assets/references/homepage-life-tree-v1.png`

This reference image establishes the first concrete homepage direction.

## Key Visual Principles

- The homepage is centered around a large life tree.
- The current life stage appears near the top of the tree.
- The tree should feel lush, mature, and full of leaves.
- The current stage should feel like adulthood, responsibility, productivity, and continuity.
- The presence of the next generation should be subtly reflected through richer foliage, family-related markers, and a warmer emotional atmosphere.
- The tree should feel alive and in active growth, not sparse or symbolic only.

## Layout Direction

- The tree occupies the center of the page.
- Life stages are arranged vertically.
- The user starts from the current stage.
- Scrolling downward means moving into the past.
- Chapter labels can appear on the left side.
- Current focus cards can appear on the right side.
- Event markers may appear as apples, pinned notes, small illustrated cards, or family/archive fragments.

## Visual Atmosphere

- Low-saturation beige-based palette.
- Warm paper-like background.
- Hand-drawn / watercolor / sketchbook feeling.
- Soft environmental landscapes behind the tree.
- Backgrounds change by life period and place.
- The UI should feel like a living archive, not a dashboard.

## Tree Rules

- The tree must be lush in the current life stage.
- Leaves should be abundant, especially around the current age period.
- Branches should show active growth and responsibility.
- Apples represent outcomes and events.
- Red apples represent meaningful achievements.
- Green apples represent lessons, unfinished work, or high-effort low-result periods.
- Apple size reflects importance, time investment, and life impact.

## Animation Direction

Animations should be subtle and natural.

Allowed:
- leaves gently moving
- slow parallax background movement
- soft lighting changes
- gradual reveal of branches and apples during scroll
- slight motion in small environmental details

Avoid:
- flashy transitions
- bouncing UI
- fast animation
- excessive particle effects
- tech-product style motion

## UI Notes

The interface should remain quiet.

Navigation, cards, and controls should support exploration without dominating the tree.

The user should feel like they are entering a personal world that is still growing.
