# Content Philosophy

The website is not organized primarily around articles or posts.

It is organized around life events represented as apples growing on the life tree.

The apple system is the core content structure of the entire website.

Articles, photos, projects, memories, notes, and relationships all exist inside or around apples.

The website should feel like exploring a living life archive rather than browsing a traditional blog.

---

# Core Structure

The entire website is organized around:

- Time
- Place
- People
- Events

These dimensions are interconnected through the tree system.

---

# Life Period Model

Life periods are primarily divided by place and stage of life.

Each major place represents a distinct chapter of life.

Examples:

- Beijing (0–22)
- Bangkok (23–27)
- Beijing Return (28–29)
- New York (30–33)
- Beijing Return (34–36)
- Paris (37–39)
- China Return (40–43)
- Johannesburg (44–Present)

Each period may contain:
- different environmental visuals
- different emotional tones
- different branch structures
- different apple density
- different relationship networks

---

# Tree Timeline Logic

The tree grows upward continuously.

The current age always appears near the top.

Scrolling downward moves into earlier life periods.

Tree trunk height should visually reflect the actual duration of time periods.

Longer periods occupy more vertical space.

Shorter periods occupy less.

---

# Apple System

Apples represent meaningful life events, outcomes, experiences, transitions, or consequences.

Each apple acts as a portal into a deeper memory or story page.

---

## Red Apple

Represents:
- achievements
- positive outcomes
- meaningful progress
- successful projects
- valuable relationships
- personal growth

---

## Green Apple

Represents:
- lessons
- uncertainty
- unfinished work
- failed experiments
- high-effort low-result periods
- unresolved situations

---

## Gift

Represents:
- major life opportunities
- unexpected turning points
- meaningful rewards
- life-changing encounters
- gifts earned through long-term effort

Visual symbol:
🎁

---

## Rotten Apple

Represents:
- irreversible failures
- dead ends
- deeply painful outcomes
- life-level mistakes
- events with permanent consequences

Rotten apples should be visually rare and emotionally heavy.

---

# Apple Size Logic

Apple size represents:
- emotional impact
- life importance
- time investment
- long-term influence

Large apples represent major life chapters.

Small apples represent smaller events or temporary experiences.

---

# Apple Detail Pages

Each apple opens into its own dedicated page.

An apple page may contain:
- writing
- photos
- essays
- journals
- projects
- notes
- memories
- media
- related people
- related events

Every apple page should feel visually unique while still belonging to the same world.

Different apples may reflect different emotional and regional atmospheres.

---

# Visibility System

Content can exist in two visibility layers:

## Public

Visible to all visitors.

Examples:
- research
- essays
- AI work
- automation
- learning
- observations

---

## Private

Requires password access.

Examples:
- family memories
- personal archives
- sensitive experiences
- intimate life records

The private layer should feel respectful and integrated naturally into the world.

---

# Place Representation

Places are not standalone content hubs.

Places primarily exist as environmental atmospheres surrounding life periods.

Examples:
- landscapes
- architecture
- lighting
- climate
- color palette
- emotional tone

The regional atmosphere should subtly shape:
- backgrounds
- illustrations
- environmental storytelling
- visual mood

---

# People System

People are represented as surrounding trees within the larger life forest.

Family and close relationships are extremely important but should not dominate the homepage directly.

People exploration may eventually exist as:
- a relationship map
- an expandable tree network
- a visual forest system

Users may navigate through connected people and relationships interactively.

---

# Article Relationship Model

Articles are not the primary structure.

Articles exist inside apples.

An apple may contain:
- one article
- multiple essays
- media collections
- journals
- notes
- project documentation

Different apples may use completely different page layouts depending on the nature of the event.

The apple is the gateway.

The content inside adapts to the memory or story being told.

---

# Long-Term Content Goal

The website should gradually evolve into a lifelong interconnected archive of:
- places
- memories
- people
- successes
- failures
- transitions
- experiments
- family
- learning
- growth

The system should remain expandable over many decades without losing emotional coherence.
